Think–Pair–Share Questions
❓ What is a circuit? click to reveal →
A circuit is a complete, closed loop that allows electricity to flow from a power source, through components (like a light bulb), and back again. If the loop is broken anywhere, electricity stops flowing.
❓ What happens if the circuit is broken? click to reveal →
The electricity stops flowing. Any components in the circuit (like bulbs or motors) turn off. This is the same principle as a light switch — it "breaks" the circuit to turn the light off.
❓ Where does electricity come from? click to reveal →
Electricity can be generated from many energy sources: batteries (chemical energy), solar panels (light energy), wind turbines (kinetic energy), and power plants (burning coal, gas, or nuclear reactions). In science, we often use batteries.
❓ What types of energy does electricity transform into? click to reveal →
Electricity can be transformed into: Light energy (light bulb), Heat energy (toaster), Sound energy (speaker), Kinetic (movement) energy (electric motor). This is called energy transformation.

🌱 Sustainability Connection
🌿 Why might we want to use renewable energy sources in circuits? click to reveal →
Renewable sources like solar and wind generate electricity without releasing greenhouse gases or using up finite resources like coal or oil. Using renewable energy in circuits (e.g., solar-powered devices) reduces environmental impact and connects to sustainable living — like the ideas in our school sustainability program!

Phase 2: Explicit Teaching

10 minutes  ·  Key vocabulary  ·  Demonstration
10 MIN ▾
Key Vocabulary
Conductor
Allows electricity to flow through it easily. Usually metals.
💡 Bulb result:  BRIGHT
Examples: copper wire, aluminium foil, coins, metal spoon, paperclip
Insulator
Blocks electricity from flowing through it. Protects us from electric shocks.
💡 Bulb result:  OFF (no light)
Examples: rubber band, plastic ruler, fabric, wood, glass
Resistor
Slows down the flow of electricity. Reduces the current in a circuit.
💡 Bulb result:  DIM
Examples: pencil (graphite), some types of carbon materials

Our Inquiry Question Which materials allow electricity to flow, and which stop or slow it?
Materials per group

  • Test items: metal spoon, paperclip, rubber band, plastic ruler, pencil (graphite), coin, fabric, aluminium foil
